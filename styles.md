## Industry Design Patterns (from 20+ car detailing websites)

### Color Palettes -- What Works

| Pattern | Usage | Examples |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Black/dark bg + white text** | ~70% of premium sites | Most common, makes car photos pop |
| **Gold/amber accents** | ~40% | Luxury feel, pairs with dark backgrounds |
| **Blue/cyan accents** | ~35% | Modern/tech feel |
| **Red accents on dark** | ~20% | Aggressive/sporty feel |

**Recommended for CODE:** Dark background (#0a0a0a to #0D0D12) + white/ivory text + gold/champagne accent (#C9A84C or #b8a265)

### Typography Patterns

- **Sans-serif dominates entirely** across premium detailing sites
- Popular fonts: Montserrat, Inter, Josefin Sans, Open Sans, Bebas, Oswald
- **Heavyweight display** (700-900) for headings + light body (300-400)
- **Uppercase treatment** very common for headlines and service names
- **Tight letter-spacing** (-0.5px to -1px) for premium feel
- **Large scale hierarchy:** H1 = 60px-96px on desktop
- **Exception for CODE:** serif italic (Playfair Display) for dramatic/luxury headlines works well as a differentiator -- most competitors don't use serif at all

### Hero Section Patterns

1. **Dark overlay on full-bleed imagery** (most common) -- 50-60% opacity dark overlay
2. **Video/animated backgrounds** -- looping detailing process video
3. **Parallax slider** -- multiple hero images with depth effect
4. **Centered bold text** with 1-2 CTAs is standard
5. **Dual CTA pattern:** "Book Now" (filled) + "Our Services" (outlined/ghost)

### Layout Patterns

- Full-bleed alternating dark/darker sections for visual rhythm
- Card-based service displays (2-3 columns) with image, title, description, price
- **Before/after galleries** -- nearly universal, critical trust builder
- Fixed/sticky nav with transparent-to-solid scroll transition
- Testimonial carousels with star ratings
- Quote request forms prominently featured on dark backgrounds
- 12-column responsive grids

### Photography Style

- **Dark, moody, high-contrast** (~65% of premium sites)
- Close-up detail shots of paint reflections, ceramic coatings, water beading
- Luxury vehicles (Porsche, McLaren, Mercedes, Audi) as social proof
- Before/after transformations
- Studio-quality lighting emphasizing gloss and reflection

### CTA Design

- **Pill/rounded buttons** (high border-radius) most popular
- Contrasting accent color against dark backgrounds
- **Ghost/outlined buttons** as secondary CTAs (white border, transparent fill)
- Most common labels: "Book Now", "Get a Quote", "Get Started"
- Phone number prominently displayed in header

### Animation Trends

- Scroll-triggered reveals (IntersectionObserver / AOS / GSAP ScrollTrigger)
- Fade-in and slide-in on section entry
- Parallax scrolling on hero
- Hover zoom on service/portfolio images
- Smooth CSS transitions with cubic-bezier easing
- Carousel/slider for testimonials and hero images

## Key Takeaways for CODE Detailing Website

1. **Dark backgrounds are the industry standard** for premium positioning -- makes car photography pop and conveys polished results
2. **Gold/champagne on black** is the strongest luxury combination (already aligned with CODE brand)
3. **Serif italic for drama headlines** differentiates CODE from competitors who all use sans-serif
4. **Before/after galleries** are critical trust builders -- must include
5. **Dual CTA approach** (Book Now + Learn More) performs best
6. **Scroll-triggered animations** add premium feel without overwhelming
7. **Close-up detail photography** of paint, ceramic beading, and reflections sells the craft
8. **Phone/WhatsApp prominently displayed** -- important for local Croatian market
9. **Video backgrounds** in hero sections are becoming standard for premium sites
10. **Testimonials with star ratings** build trust for a premium-priced local service
